Service



Giving back to my community is very important to me. Ever since I was young, I was always taught the value and importance of selfless service. In high school, I was apart of multiple service organizations including Student Council, Interact, Junior Chamber of Commerce, National Honor Society, and Gentlemen's League. During my time in these organizations, I helped put on large-scale events such as Red Ribbon Week, Operation Christmas Child, and Purple Pinky. I also helped facilitate some local events such as singing Christmas carols to senior living homes, occasionally cleaning up a highway for Adopt-a-Highway, canned food/clothes drive during the holidays, and serving at local banquets for charity.



In my time in college, I have continued to give my time to my new community in Aggieland. Some of my collegiate volunteer work includes going and helping at the Howdy Farm on campus and going to elementary schools to play with children until their parents arrive. I also have participated in the Big Event my freshmen year, helped clean up Kyle Field after a home football game, and have spent some time volunteering at the Brazos Valley Food Bank. Some ways that I plan on giving back to my community this year is to start volunteering my time at the local Boys & Girls Club in the Bryan College Station area. Being in the Boys & Girls Club back home was an enormous part of my development as a child, and I believe that me giving back to this organization will be beneficial to the youth and myself. I also plan on joining the collegiate version of Interact, Rotaract, at Texas A&M. To be in an organization of people who love to serve others would be an awesome experience that I can not wait for.
